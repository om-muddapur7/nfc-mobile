Nugikeri Hanuman Temple
Lord Hanuman

Nuggikeri Hanuman Temple (Kannada: ನುಗ್ಗಿಕೇರಿ ಶ್ರೀ ಹನುಮಾನ್ ದೇವಸ್ಥಾನ) is one of the oldest and popular Hindu temples in Dharwad, in the state of Karnataka, India. The presiding deity of the temple is Lord Hanuman and the temple complex also houses other deities viz. Lord Rama, Lord Shiva, Goddess Saraswathi, Goddess Durga, Goddess Santoshimata, Lord Venugopala Swamy, and Lord Jagannath. The temple is located at the airport road and 18 km away from central Hubli, Dharwad, Karnataka, India. Due to Vishesha Divasa for Lord Hanuman this place gets crowded every Saturday and on Hanuman Jayanti.

Brief history of Temple

The history of Nugikeri Hanuman Temple, located in Dharwad, Karnataka, India, is steeped in spiritual significance and folklore:

Mythological origins
According to local legends, the temple was established by the Pandavas from the Mahabharata during their exile. It is believed that Hanuman, the powerful monkey god, appeared before the Pandavas at this spot, leading them to install an idol of him as a symbol of strength and devotion. The temple is dedicated to Lord Hanuman, who is revered for his unwavering devotion to Lord Rama, and is considered a site of divine blessings and protection.

Construction
The origins of the temple are believed to date back several centuries, with local traditions attributing its founding to the time of the Pandavas. Over the years, the temple has undergone several renovations. The present structure was restored and expanded by local rulers and devotees over time, maintaining its traditional Dravidian architecture, with a large, centrally located idol of Lord Hanuman.

Location
Nugikeri Hanuman Temple is situated in the Nugikeri village, about 15 kilometers from Dharwad. It attracts devotees from all over Karnataka and beyond, especially during Hanuman Jayanti. The temple is nestled amidst a serene environment, surrounded by greenery, which adds to its peaceful and spiritual ambiance.



Best time to get here

The ideal time to visit the Nugikeri Hanuman Temple is during the post-monsoon and winter months, between September and February:

Weather
The weather during these months is pleasant, with cool temperatures ranging from 15–25°C, making it comfortable for devotees to perform their rituals and prayers. Monsoons can bring heavy rains, while summers can get quite hot in Dharwad.

Activities
During Hanuman Jayanti, which typically falls between March and April, the temple hosts large gatherings of devotees who come to offer special prayers and participate in celebrations. The peaceful surroundings also make it a popular spot for meditation and spiritual retreats throughout the year.
